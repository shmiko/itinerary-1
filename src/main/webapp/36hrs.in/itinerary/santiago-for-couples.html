Santiago for Couples

Santiago

 by Patrick Hieger
$$
romantic
sightseeing

In the last couple of years there has been a decent amount of press about actually discovering Santiago and not simply using it as a stopping point on the way to the Patagonia or the Atacama desert. Not just for couples clad in brown hiking boots and nylon cargo pants, there’s plenty to see and do around Santiago for couples young and old. From good food and art to keepsake shopping, visitors can a get a taste of the city from east to west.

Friday

Hotel Su Merced
thumbnails
calendar

6:00pm

Check in at Su Merced (Merced 120), Santiago's newest boutique hotel that, outside either entrance, has it all. From views of Parque Forestal and the famed heladería Emporio La Rosa to the main entrance that's just steps from Plaza Italia, this affordable, centrally located, modern boutique is the perfect getaway for a long weekend.

Centro Gabriela Mistral
thumbnails
calendar

8:00pm

Pass through the up-and-coming Lastarria neighborhood on your way to the GAM (Avenida Libertador Bernardo O'Higgins 227). Once a headquarters for Pinochet’s regime before it mysteriously burned down in 2005, the GAM is now Santiago’s premiere cultural arts center. With a library stocked full of art, design and culture books, as well as various galleries and theaters, GAM is a one-stop shop for all of your cultural needs. Each weekend they feature new performances from theatre to dance, as well as art openings and other related events. There’s also a café, should you need a shot of caffeine for the rest of the evening.

Wind your way back through Lastarria on your way to the multi-leveled Opera/Catedrál (Merced 395), one of Santiago’s hottest dining spots. Located at the corner of Merced and Jose Miguel de la Barra, try to snag a seat on the roof deck and enjoy views of Cerro Santa Lucia by night, as well as the bustling Bellas Artes neighborhood. Try Opera’s modern take on classic Chilean ingredients like Merluza, Lamb, and market-fresh vegetables. Wash it all down with a glass of carmenére, Chile’s most recognized grape.

Saturday

ColmadoCoffee

10:00am

Head out the park entrance of Su Merced and just down the street you’ll find Colmado Coffee (Merced 346), the clandestine coffee shop run by a Spaniard-Chilean couple. There you’ll find the weekends-only brunch of dishes like tortilla with chorizo and other Spanish favorites, paired with a skillfully prepared coffee infusion.

Head over to the Baquedano metro stop and take the red line all the way east to the Los Dominicos stop. Once you exit the metro, head towards the mountains and find Los Dominicos (Avenida Apoquindo 9085), Santiago’s most beloved artisan market. Housed in an old mission at the foot of the mountains, Los Dominicos is chock full of handmade leather goods, religious objects, knitted sweaters, and so on. If brunch didn’t tide you over, enjoy some fresh calzones rotos (literally torn panties), kind of like Chile’s version of funnel cake, cooked fresh at the market’s café. When you’re finished shopping, head into the chapel and get a taste of how Chile used to look. Still not satisfied? Enjoy the farmer’s market just outside of the metro that takes place every Saturday.

Liguria
thumbnails
calendar

1:00pm

Via metro, head back to the Providencia neighborhood to the Manuel Montt metro stop. There you’ll find Liguria (Providencia 1373), easily the most famous restaurant in Santiago. Chock full of old signs, black and white photos, a generous wine selection and more art than your eye’s can handle, Liguria is always buzzing. Cheers over a pisco sour, then enjoy Italian favorites like gnocchi or raviolis stuffed with cheese.

Parque Forestal
thumbnails
calendar

2:30pm

Walk off your abundant lunch as you stroll through Parque Forestal on your way back to the Bellas Artes neighborhood. Just three metro stops or a 40-minute walk, Parque Forestal is full of couples young and old quite publicly displaying their affections. Groups of ‘football’ players will be trying to outdo each other in pick-up games and bike riders will languidly pedal along the groomed paths.

Museo Nacional De Bellas Artes
thumbnails
calendar

4:00pm

Once your walk has ended and you find yourself back in Bellas Artes, head to either side of the museum for a retrospective of Chilean art. In the Museo Nacional side, just on Jose Miguel de la Barra, you’ll find various rotating exhibits. On the backside, at the contemporary art museum, you’ll find not only modern Chilean art, but some of the best artists the international scene has to offer. Afterwards, head to Lastarria and enjoy the MAVI. This stark, highly modern gallery-like museum features cutting edge art and has a great museum store to match.

Once you leave the MAVI, head over to the Cerro Santa Lucía and watch the sunset. The first outpost of Spanish settlers, now the hill is a maze of paths that wind to the top of the former fortress. From the top you can get a proper 360 view of Santiago.

After a day spent seeing the sights and buying trinkets, you should dine like a Chilean native at modern Mapuche restaurant Peumayen (Constitución 136). Located on the northern side of the Mapocho river in Santiago’s famed Bellavista neighborhood, Peumayen is one of the hottest new additions to the Santiago culinary landscape. The bread service is a board of indigenous breads found throughout Chile, and the appetizer sampler is incredible.

The White Rabbit
thumbnails
calendar

10:00pm

After sampling the old world in Santiago, head over to The White Rabbit (Antonia López de Bello 118) and get a taste of what gringo drinks look and taste like south of the equator. There you’ll find upgraded takes on classic Chilean drinks, as well as proper, modern cocktails. Beware, though--one too many and you just might end up chasing the white rabbit!

Sunday

Casa Museo La Chascona
thumbnails
calendar

11:00am

Before heading out of town, get to know a little more about famed Chilean poet, Pablo Neruda. Located at the foot of Cerro San Cristóbal in Bellavista, La Chascona (Fernando Márquez de La Plata 0192) was once Neruda’s home, and now one of the most interesting museums Santiago has to offer. Take a guided tour and learn some interesting facts and eccentricities about one of Chile’s most famous writers.
