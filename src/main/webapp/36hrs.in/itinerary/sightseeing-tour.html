Sightseeing Tour

Lima

 by Ed Stocker
$$
sightseeing
cultural

Peru’s capital may often be overcast, due to a sea fog that rolls in from the Pacific, but overlook it at your peril. The culinary capital of South America is packed full of colonial gems and pre-Columbian treasures. And then there’s the pisco-fuelled nightlife…

Friday

Plaza Mayor de Lima
thumbnails
calendar

5:00pm

First stop on your Lima checklist should be the Plaza Mayor (also known as the Plaza de Armas), the city’s birthplace and still by far its most impressive square. A colonial gem – and a Unesco world heritage site – the northern side is dominated by the government palace, the official presidential residence. At 5.45pm you can the changing of the guard. On the eastern side is the impressive 16th century Cathedral. Keep the religious theme heading east a couple of blocks to the St Francis Convent Museum (Plazuela San Francisco) which has some great works of art by the likes of Rubens and Van Dyck and some grizzly catacombs packed full of skeletons.

All that religion and skeleton viewing probably means you’re in need of a stiff drink. Pisco sour, a potent grape spirit mixed with lemon, is Peru’s most famous drink and nowhere does it better than within the gentrified confines of the Gran Hotel Bolivar’s El Bolivarcito bar (Jr. de la Unión 926).

Peru’s gastronomic revolution is beginning to make its mark around the world; but it began here. High-end restaurants range from the innovative eco-friendly Nanka run by an Australian-Peruvian couple in the La Molina neighborhood (Jr. Bambúes 198) to the original restaurant from the godfather of limeña cuisine Gastón Acurio, Astrid & Gastón (Canturias 175). But for those on a more limited budget his Tanta chain is more affordable and equally tasty, with a restaurant located just off the square (Pasaje Nicolás de Rivera El Viejo 142).

Yacana Bar
thumbnails
calendar

10:00pm

Head to Yacana (Jr. de la Union 892, Floor 2, Sat & Sun from 8pm), a bohemian bar with second floor views looking out onto the historic Plaza San Martín. This is one of central Lima’s classic night life joints and alongside the eclectic mix of music – anything from contemporary rock to 80s classics – the bar has an art gallery and often puts on cultural events.

Continue the day as you mean to go on with a bit of extreme sport in the form of tandem paragliding over the upmarket barrio of Miraflores (Parque Raimondi, near the lighthouse, several schools fly here including Aeroextreme (01 242 5125). A pretty fine way to get an aerial view of the city and clear the head.

Head down to La Máquina Café & Cultura (Alcanfores 323) for a hearty set-lunch menu or one of their towering salads. This is the original hipster hangout in Miraflores, with colorful walls, comfy sofas and stacks of magazines and books. Or head to a nearby café serving ceviche – a classic lunchtime staple of fish cooked in lime juicy, served with sweet potato and toasted corn.

The adjoining neighborhood of Barranco is Peru’s artistic enclave, a one-time seaside resort of the elite. The newest gallery on the scene is the Asociación Mario Testino, known as MATE (Avenida Pedro de Osma 409), the first permanent collection of the legendary Lima-born, London-based fashion photographer. It’s also worth also checking out the Pedro de Osma Museum (Avenida Pedro de Osma 421), just a few houses down on the same side of the road, for something completely different. The building houses ornate religious and colonial art, some dating back as far as the 16th century.

Probably the oddest place you’ll ever have dinner is Expreso Virgen de Guadalupe (Av. Prolongación San Martín 15-A), just off the main square in Barranco. Housed in a converted tram carriage, this diner does an excellent vegetarian buffet and there’s usually live music from 10pm. A fun, intimate setting.

Sunday

Kio

9:30am

Kio café (Av. Universitaria 871, San Miguel) doesn’t look like anything special, a spin’n’sawdust eatery popular with locals. But it also happens to do the best pan con chicharrones de cerdo (a fried pork sandwich) in town, a traditional Sunday breakfast that isn’t for the faint-hearted.

Huaca Pucllana
thumbnails
calendar

11:00am

Peru is dotted with ancient historical sites and Lima is no different. There are a handful of huacas (temple ruins) in the capital, one of the most accessible being Huaca Pucllana (General Borgoño, block 8, 01 617 7138) in Miraflores. It also has an excellent restaurant attached if you want a final gastronomic treat.
