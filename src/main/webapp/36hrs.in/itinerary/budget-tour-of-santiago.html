Budget Tour of Santiago

Santiago

 by Patrick Hieger
$
budget
sightseeing

As it turns out, Santiago, Chile, isn’t a bad place to be if you’re traveling on a budget. There’s plenty of things to do for free, and eating can sometimes be cheaper than public transportation.

Friday

Hostal de la Barra www.hostaldelabarra.com
thumbnails
calendar

6:00pm

There’s nothing that says even though you’re on a budget you can’t have a comfortable stay in a cool environment. Hostal de la Barra (José Miguel de la Barra 451, Bellas Artes) is a former apartment building turned hostel, is directly in the middle of two of Santiago’s neighborhoods and literally on top of great restaurants, cafes and bustling night life. The Café de la Barra on the ground floor is a great place for a quick breakfast of coffee and snacks, and they sell beer, in case you need an afternoon relaxer.

HBH
thumbnails
calendar

8:00pm

Because it is currently only accessible by vehicles on four wheels, Santiago’s Ñuñoa hasn’t appeared in a lot of write-ups as of late. Neighborhoods like Providencia and Lastarria get all the lime light, but Ñuñoa is, hands down, one of Santiago’s top five neighborhoods. This is due, in large part, to the fact that it houses HBH (Av. Irarrázaval 3176, Ñuñoa), a pizzeria and micro brew. Head over on a Friday night and squeeze yourself into a table. It’s first come first served, but once you sit down, try each of the three beers they make in house, as well as one of the various pizzas. HBH is right near Plaza Ñuñoa, so you can get there via the 505, which runs along Merced just outside of the Hostal de la Barra. You can take it back, too.

Saturday

Plaza de Armas
thumbnails
calendar

10:00am

Just an 8-block walk from the hostel, Santiago’s Plaza de Armas is ground zero for a Chilean culture lesson. Home to the national post office, former political offices, the national cathedral and monuments and maps that highlight a good portion of the country’s founding, the Plaza de Armas is a one-stop shop for history buffs. Not to be forgotten, the Plaza is also home to “completo row”, a block-long row of cheap hot dog stands and diners that sell one of Chile’s national dishes, the completo. Sidle up to any one of the eateries and eat like a local as you enjoy hot dogs smothered in avocado, tomato and mayonnaise. On a given day they might sell over 10,000 hot dogs.

Palacio de La Moneda
thumbnails
calendar

11:00am

Head southeast from the Plaza de Armas and you’ll run into the national palace, La Moneda. You can’t get in, but a good walk around the entire building is like stepping back in history. Long since restored after the military coup of 1973, the palace is a beautiful building. Below its southernmost courtyard, too, it also houses one of Santiago’s best art galleries.

One wish that Santiago visitors always have is to be able to get to the mountains. While they are close, getting truly up close and personal is a bit of an expensive task. For the budget-conscious traveler, though, the Cajon de Maipo is just a short ride bus away. Take the green Metro Line 5 to Bellavista de La Florida and there you’ll find the Metrobus No. 72. You can take this all the way up to San José de Maipo, the small town that acts as the jumping off point for the canyon itself. For a short day, you can tour the small village, and even walk just outside of town and find yourself in some scenic country. It’s not the Andes proper, but a taste nonetheless.

Barrio Bellas Artes
thumbnails
calendar

7:00pm

From San José de Maipo you can take the same bus back into town. Take the green metro line back to Bellas Artes, and catch a cat nap at the hostel before you head out.

Barrio Brasil
thumbnails
calendar

9:00pm

Take the metro from Bellas Artes to Cumming, and get ready for a fun night out. In Barrio Brasil you’ll find a host of places to eat, drink and experience Chilean night life. Hungry? Grab an empanada. Thirsty? Look for a Kuntsmann or Austral sign outside of one of the numerous bars and restaurants and try some of Chile’s finest microbrews. If you’re out past 11:30, you’ll need to take a cab home. Don’t worry, though, it’ll only run you about $8.00 US.

Sunday

Cerro San Cristobal
thumbnails
calendar

10:00am

Before you leave Santiago, you should definitely get a bird’s eye view of the city. If you head up Pio Nono in Santiago’s Bellavista neighborhood. Once you hit the entrance to Cerro San Cristobal, you can take the funicular, a sort of train / trolley that crawls up the side of the hill, all the way to the top. There you can get a proper 360 view of the city, and pay homage to the statue of the Virgin Mary that sits on top. A little blessing before heading out never hurt anyone.
