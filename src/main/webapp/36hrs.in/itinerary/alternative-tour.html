Alternative Tour

Lima

 by Walter Rhein
$$
sightseeing
cultural

This tour is for those who are at the end of their vacation in Peru and are burned out on visiting traditional touristy things. Rather than check out more museums and traditional sites, perhaps you'd prefer to lounge by a pool and play some traditional Peruvian lawn games. This tour will take you to the outskirts of Lima where the residents of the city of kings go to relax after a long work week. These 36 hours will allow you to test your Spanish skills and see parts of Peru that most tourists never experience.

Mavery is well known to Peruvians, but it's not quite as famous on an international scale as places like Astrid & Gaston, or Rafael. Still, the warm ambiance, and romantic atmosphere are not to be missed. Specializing in pizza, empanadas, and pasta, Mavery is sure to provide you with a night of fine dining for a very reasonable price (around $20 per person). Live music is also featured on a regular basis, check the web page for updates.

Sargento Pimienta
thumbnails
calendar

10:00pm

Baranco is known as Lima's most bohemian district, and was recently named as one of the most colorful neighborhoods in South America. Get a feel for it as you walk through the central plaza at night, and don't forget to take a hike out to the famous “puente de suspiros” (bridge of sighs). If you feel like dancing, there are plenty of clubs to be found throughout the small district. Live music is almost always featured at Sargento Pimiento.

Saturday

Jockey Plaza
thumbnails
calendar

9:00am

Cieneguilla is one of the fringe districts of Lima. It is notable because the constant cloud of fog that hangs over Lima is rarely present. As a result, many Limenos head to Cieneguilla frequently to catch some rays and play in the well-tended grassy areas that can be found there. It's a wonderful place to relax, but getting there is a little bit of a trick.

The many taxi drivers of Lima will be reluctant to go to Cieneguilla because of the uncertainty of returning with a fare. However, if you take a taxi to San Borja, you will be able to find a taxi willing to take you to Cieneguilla from there (there are always plenty of taxis waiting around at Jockey Plaza).

If you're feeling more adventurous, you can take a collective taxi to Cieneguilla. These taxis conglomerate at the corner of Morelli (the parallel road to Aviacion) and Javier Prado. The price is significantly lower, but the collective taxis don't leave for Cieneguilla until they are full. However, they will set off if you agree to pay for any empty seats.

El Colono Inn
thumbnails
calendar

11:00am

The trip from the San Borja district of Lima to Cieneguilla takes about an hour and a half, and it's a nice way to see the outlying terrain of Lima. Be warned, the roads are a bit windy and the drivers like to go very fast, so you might be a bit dizzy upon arrival.

Cieneguilla has a ton of inexpensive mini-resorts to choose from. Prices are generally about S/. 50 to S/. 60 per night. My personal preference is the Colono inn. Generally rooms are available but it's worth calling in advance since they sometimes have weekend events.

Upon arrival, the resort starts a bill and you pay when you leave. The food at Colono is excellent, although it may not have the emphasis on presentation that you get at some of Lima's most famous eateries. The meat dishes are especially good. Another resort of comparable value is the Sol y Luna.

Sapo
thumbnails
calendar

2:00pm

Sapo is a traditional Peruvian lawn game that is similar to “washers” or “bean bag toss.” You are required to toss small bronze coins at the mouth of a bronze frog that sits on a box. The box has holes cut into the top, and you gain points by having your coin fall through the holes or go into the frog's mouth. Playing Sapo is quite addictive, and it is also a fun, casual way to meet some local people as they'll be only too delighted to come and play with you.

Horseback riding, swimming, sunbathing, hiking

As a resort area, there are plenty of activities you can take part in. The local mountains are fun for quick hikes, and there are other resorts nearby that offer horseback riding. Or perhaps you'll just want to relax and sample the various menu items.

Mesa de Piedra
thumbnails
calendar

8:00pm

Any of the resorts in Cineguilla have excellent food, but the most famous restaurant in the area is Mesa de Piedra. Like the name implies, you will be seated at large stone tables and served a tremendous, traditionally cooked, Peruvian meal. Take note that when the sun goes down it can become rather cool in Cineguilla, so bring a sweatshirt or light jacket.

Note: if you stay out late, don't be surprised if the gate is closed and locked when you return to your resort. The gates are always closed for your security, simply ring the bell and someone will open for you.

Sunday

san borja
thumbnails

9:30am

In the morning you will be able to order any one of several fine breakfast options before heading back to the San Borja district of Lima. After checking out of the resort, you will have to wait outside and watch for vehicles to pass. Often, taxis will put stickers in their windows that say “Lima Collectivo” and will stop when you flag them down. From San Borja, you can get another taxi to anywhere in Lima you wish to go.
