Mumbai Luxe $$$

Mumbai

 by bombayjules
$$$
luxury
shopping

Mumbai has more than its fair share of billionaires – and they need to be catered to somehow!   In the city, you will find many shops, restaurants and hotels fulfilling the every need of the super rich and the discerning traveller - all you need is the time!

Friday

Taj Mahal Palace & Tower
thumbnails
calendar

3:00pm

Check into Mumbai’s most luxurious (as well as most famous) hotel – The Taj Mahal Palace & Tower on Apollo Bunder.  Soon after, go downstairs and check out the Luxury Shopping Boulevard where you can take your pick of fine gems at Gazdar, handbags and scarves at Louis Vuitton and Dior and Mont Blanc pens (amongst others).

Sea Lounge, Taj Mahal Palace Hotel
thumbnails
calendar

5:00pm

There is nothing for it – you’ll need a cup of tea after blowing the budget on that dream Dior handbag or wallet.  Head back up to the Sea Lounge and indulge yourselves in an Afternoon Tea – Taj style.

The Table
thumbnails
calendar

9:00pm

After a luxurious soak in your Taj Palace bath, a spruce and a preen, take yourselves off to The Table for cocktails followed by a relaxing dinner.  One of Mumbai’s top restaurants, you will find much to tickle your taste buds from the selection of ‘small plates’ and ‘large plates’.  Amongst others, try the Boneless Chicken Wings – miraculously fashioned into tiny, crispy squares; or the fashionable Crispy Pork Belly Sliders. Round off your meal with the heavenly and indulgent sounding Salted Caramel Ice Cream Sundae….ooh la la!

You can head back to your hotel after dinner, perhaps enjoying a cognac at the cocktail bar before returning to your room. Do enjoy plunging your woozy head into those soft, feather pillows!

Saturday

LPQ
thumbnails
calendar

9:30am

Of course – there is no need to rush off!  Take your pick from the Taj’s breakfast buffet and read a newspaper. Your car isn’t coming until 11.00 to whisk you off to your next appointment. (Alternatively for breakfast - sample a delicious Belgian style breakfast at the delectable Le Pain Quotidien in Colaba – around the corner from the Taj on CSM Road and right near the Gateway to India. Choose from omelettes, waffles, porridge, pastries, etc.)

Artisans'
thumbnails
calendar

11:00am

And now for some culture! Ask your driver to take you to the art district Kala Ghoda (a short drive away) where you will visit a couple of galleries.  The first is the recently opened Mumbai outpost of The Delhi Art Gallery (58 Dr VB Gandhi Mark, Rhythm House Lane) where you can view four floors of works by inonic Indian artists such as MF Husain, FN Souza, KH Ara, SK Bakre, Tyeb Mehta, Akbar Padamsee and Ram Kumar.  Enquire after the prices …if you dare.

Next stop is the Artisans’ Centre – a few doors down and upstairs. (at number 52-56 Dr VB Gandhi Marg).  The exhibition here changes constantly but you may be able to pick yourself up a nice artefact or two.

When entering the Artisans’ Centre, you won’t have failed to notice Sabyasachi’s store on the ground floor.  Enter this dark, beautiful space and take your pick of bejewelled anarkalis and sarees from India’s leading fashion designer.

Nico Bombay
thumbnails
calendar

1:00pm

Turn left out of Sabyasachi or the Artisans’ Centre and walk down the street a little further.  (No point taking the car but you can have your driver wait if so desired) Turn left again on to Apollo Street and you will see on the right, the sign for Nico Bombay.  Time for lunch!  Opened in October 2013, this is the newest kid on Mumbai’s gourmet block.  Meat lovers should start their meal with the veal tenderloin, follow it with the sumptuous duck confit and polish it off with the chocolate fondant – all the while enjoying the New York ambience (and a couple of glasses of Grover Viognier). 

Good Earth
thumbnails
calendar

3:00pm

And now for shopping heaven!  Get your driver to head back in the direction of the Taj Palace hotel and ask him to locate Good Earth on BEST Marg – Mumbai’s most stylish interiors store.  Roam amongst the highly patterned cushions and bedding and pick your way through the colourful crockery and gorgeous bathroom products.  You will definitely come away with something lovely from here!  Then go next door to Bombay Electric for your quirky designer clothing and one-off gift ideas.

Get back in the car and take the short hop to Phillips Antiques opposite the Regal Cinema.  Here you will find a finely curated selection of antiques and artefacts, silver, pottery, traditional handcrafted items and more. 

Want to do more clothes shopping?  Then you must go to Ensemble (‘India’s first haute couture store’) in the Great Western Building opposite Lion Gate (a few minutes away).  Browse such labels as Monica Jaisingh, Sabyasachi (again), Varun Bahl and Tarun Tahiliani. 

Taj Mahal Palace & Tower
thumbnails
calendar

6:00pm

After three hours of shopping therapy, it’s time to go back to your hotel and enjoy the comforts of your room or the hotel lounge (perhaps ordering yourself a nice pot of Darjeeling tea).  Get ready for the next instalment – and why not slip into that new Sabyasachi dress whilst you’re at it?

Aer at Four Seasons Hotel Mumbai
thumbnails
calendar

7:30pm

Be ready for your driver to transport you to Aer – one of Mumbai’s most glamorous drinking spots at the top of The Four Seasons Hotel in Lower Parel (the cover charge is 2,500 Rs for non residents on Fridays and Saturdays from 8.00 pm - but who’s counting?).  You might even be in time to catch the end of the sunset if you’re lucky.

shiro
thumbnails
calendar

9:00pm

People in Mumbai tend to eat very late – so if you’re still happily drinking and chatting away in Aer, you can go even later to Shiro’s – the chic Oriental restobar at Bombay Dyeing Mills Compound nearby. Here on the mezzanine – you can indulge in nicely executed sushi, rice, noodles or any of the other Asian fusion dishes whilst tapping your foot to the DJ-led music entertaining people in the bar below.

Sunday

L'Occitane
thumbnails
calendar

10:00am

After another stupendous buffet breakfast (or even room service?) ask to be transported to the French ‘Spa La Vie’ by L’Occitane back in Lower Parel.  At this luxurious 8,000 sq ft standalone facility, you can complete your weekend away by enjoying ‘soaking ceremonies and emollient properties in on of the free standing bathtubs and private steam chambers in every treatment room’. If that along doesn’t sound wonderful enough, you can indulge in one of the many hammam, massage, facial and beauty treatments available.  Treatments range between 650 Rs for a 15 minute Soothing Provencal Steam to 9,500 Rs for a 2 hour Provencal Detoxifying Hammam.
