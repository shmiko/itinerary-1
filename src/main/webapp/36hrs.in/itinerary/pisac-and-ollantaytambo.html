Pisac and Ollantaytambo

Cusco

 by Walter Rhein
$$
outdoors
sightseeing

Cusco is one of the jewels of South America with a plethora of archeological sites and activities right on its doorstep. It is the gateway to the sacred valley, and it is possible to take some tremendous day trips that will provide you with photos and memories you will treasure for the rest of your life. This itinerary will detail two of the sacred valley's most spectacular ruins: Pisac and Ollantaytambo. Both of these ruins allow for the opportunity to test yourself with several hours of strenuous hiking. However, it is also possible to do a moderate tour and cut back on the required physical exertion substantially. Whatever you choose, make sure you have new batteries in your camera and space on your memory card because you'll be taking some of the best pictures of your life.

Your first night in Cusco should always be spent in easy to moderate activity to allow yourself to acclimate to the altitude. One fun activity is to hike around Cusco and familiarize yourself with the city's unique layout. Cusco was the head of the Inca Empire, and, as such, the streets are often windy and narrow, more suitable to horses and carts than vehicular traffic. However, this does not stop the local taxi drivers from hurtling down these streets, so exercise caution whenever you're out and about in Cusco.

Paddy's (124 Calle Triunfo) is a fun attraction right off the Plaza de Armas, and has become a cultural melting pot combining Irish and Peruvian culture. Boasting the title as “the highest Irish owned pub in the world,” you're sure to encounter a crowd of eager travelers from across the globe as well as a substantial number of friendly locals. This is a great place to enjoy a beer before your travels, or to celebrate them once you're nearing the journey's end.

Saturday

Plaza de Armas de Cusco
thumbnails

8:00am

You're going to want to get an early start for a day of adventure in the Sacred Valley. It's easy to find transport from Cusco to Pisac in the Plaza de Armas of Cusco. Either flag down a taxi and ask to rent it for the day, or look for a more official tour at one of the ubiquitous guide services. Taxis will be available throughout the Sacred Valley as well, but they're not as numerous as they are in Cusco, making a day hire the most convenient (this will cost you around $50). Once you've secured your ride, settle in for the spectacular trip to Pisac through the beautiful Andean mountains.

Pisac Market
thumbnails

9:00am

Pisac is one of the most beautiful sets of ruins in all of the Sacred Valley, and there are two distinct options for viewing it. You can either begin your hiking tour at the Pisac market and climb up to the top, or you can start at the top and climb back down into the valley. From the bottom, it is about a 4 hour hike to see the complete set of ruins, and it is fairly strenuous for the first hour or so. Alternatively, you can have your taxi drop you at the top and you can either walk back down to the market, or have your taxi wait for you at the upper parking lot while you do a smaller loop. Take note that this is a very large set of ruins, and although you can see the major structures in about an hour, you might find yourself compelled to explore until dark.

Scrambling around the ruins is enough to build up a substantial appetite, and a great little place to eat is the Doña Clorinda (La Rinconada) restaurant located about one and half kilometers outside of Pisac on the Carretera Pisac. This is a rustic little restaurant which will provide you with a good, inexpensive, traditional Peruvian meal. When you've finished eating, make sure you take a little time to walk through the Pisac market which is among the best markets in all of the sacred valley. Alternatively, you can find several dining options within Pisac itself.

From Pisac it is about an hour drive to Ollantaytambo. The road follows the beautiful Urubamba river at the heart of the Sacred Valley. Keep your camera ready as you roll through a host of quaint little villages along the way. The main ruins of Ollantaytambo are small enough to see in a couple of hours, however, there are enough alternative hiking options to make Ollantaytambo a destination point for an entire day. If you are feeling tired from the Pisac hike, you can just relax in the small village of Ollantaytambo where you'll encounter some wonderful dining opportunities.

There are several hostels in Ollantaytambo with prices ranging from $20 to $100 a night. Most of these can be found along the road that runs parallel to the little river that descends down to the train station. Like Cusco, there are some affordable, gourmet restaurants to be found in Ollantaytambo. Prices will be around $25 per person. A good starting place would be Orishas (Ave Ferrocarrill, No. 119, Ollantaytambo), which boasts of a wonderful view of the ruins of Ollantaytambo.

Sunday

Cusco

9:00am

In the morning, you'll be able to find comfortable, affordable return transportation to Cusco by hiking down the Ave Ferrocarrill to the train station. Just outside the train station there will be a line of buses waiting to take you back to Cusco at the price of $3-$5 per person. With these tour buses, you can sit back, relax, and return to Cusco in style.
