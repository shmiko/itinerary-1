Buenos Aires on a Shoestring

Buenos Aires

 by Paige Nichols
$
budget
cultural

Part of the allure of this South American capital city is that you can soak in the delights of European-influenced architecture and culture without the hefty price tag. Museums, events and restaurants can cater to any budget, especially those who must keep an extra eye on where their money goes. Despite the pesky inflation rate, which ensures that your café con leche today will most surely cost more in a few months' time, Buenos Aires remains an attractive destination for those with a backpacker's budget, or mindset.

Friday

El Banco Rojo
thumbnails
calendar

2:00pm

Start your weekend off in the city’s historical center, San Telmo. Cobblestone streets, crumbling buildings that speak to what was once a more distinguished era, and a bohemian atmosphere make this a wonderful barrio to get a feel for BA. Once you’ve worked up an appetite, stop off at El Banco Rojo for a tasty bite that won’t break the bank. Try their abundant salads, spicy wraps and other inventive dishes. Spend the rest of the afternoon stretching your legs and popping into the various shops full of antiques and curiosities. Feast your eyes at Gil Antigüedades, a masterfully curated store facing iconic Plaza Dorrego that has served as inspiration for none other than fashion designer John Galliano when he passed through town. Prices might be steep, but the fun is in ogling all that the shop has to offer.

Pagano Club Social
thumbnails
calendar

9:00pm

Take advantage of the booming supper club trend in Buenos Aires and dine at Pagano Club Social (address provided upon reservation), located in a wonderfully quirky family home (think Wes Anderson’s film The Royal Tenenbaums) in a posh area of Palermo. You’ll be greeted by both warm host Patricia and energetic cocker spaniel Tango before settling in at your table staged in the living room. Pagano Club Social is a unique opportunity to enjoy a delicious home-cooked meal in an intimate setting. The twist? It’s all a la gorra, meaning you only pay for drinks – the rest is up to you (and your inner moral compass). The excellent service and atmosphere will ensure both your generosity and plans for future visits.

Saturday

BA Free Tour
thumbnails
calendar

11:00am

Eager to see the sights like a local but can’t foot the bill of a private guide? Sign up for BA Free Tour, where you can learn the city from within, without spending a dime. Move about on public transit and soak up all that the knowledgeable guides have to offer. You’ll pass landmarks such as the Obelisk, the presidential residence known as the Pink House, the national Congress and the impressive Teatro Colón, recently renovated in 2010.

Reward your morning of exercise with the buttery, flaky goodness of the pastries at delightful café Le Blé in Chacarita. Situated on a leafy corner near the outskirts of Palermo Hollywood, this spot serves up some of the best baked goods in town alongside a tempting savory menu as well. Saddle up to the communal table and make some friends in the process, and quench your thirst with a fresh pitcher of lemonade before heading to your next destination. After eating, browse the small neighborhood shops and even pop over to the nearby Parque de los Andes for some sunshine and people watching.

Mercado de las Pulgas
thumbnails
calendar

5:00pm

Before heading back to home base for a nap, visit the Mercado de las Pulgas, the city’s large flea market. Though it’s lost a bit of its gritty charm since being moved from a somewhat more dilapidated structure across the street to a newer and more organized space, the market is full of great finds, home goods and vintage treasures. Befriend a shopkeeper or two and you’re sure to bring home a bargain.

El Obrero
thumbnails
calendar

9:00pm

A visit to Buenos Aires isn’t complete without a steak dinner, so visit a local mecca for the most authentic experience. Founded in 1954, El Obrero, located in the city’s southern La Boca neighborhood (home to both Caminito and the famous soccer team Boca Juniors), serves up delicious, no-fuss food in a laid-back neighborhood atmosphere. The menu, scrawled on chalkboards, is posted on the walls alongside dozens of flags, photos and other memorabilia. There is a special buzz in the air, so soak it up as you dive in to your massive plates of juicy steaks and crispy French fries. It doesn’t get much more authentic than this.

Sunday

Plaza Francia
thumbnails
calendar

10:30am

Take a stroll through the picturesque Recoleta neighborhood, full of elegant homes and posh storefronts, before visiting the weekly street fair that fills up Plaza Francia with both locals and tourists alike. Browse the numerous stalls for those last-minute souvenirs – you’ll have plenty from which to choose. Street performers, food vendors and other characters flood the sidewalks, making this quite the sensory experience. Buy a glass of fresh-squeezed orange juice and perch on the hill, taking in the sights and enjoying the city’s beautiful green spaces.

Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes
thumbnails
calendar

12:30pm

Just across from the fair is one of BA’s greatest gems, the Fine Arts Museum (Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes). Entry is free and provides you access to some of the most iconic works in history; take special note of Argentine artists Eduardo Mac Entyre and Clorindo Testa. There is always an excellent selection of temporary exhibits to accompany the permanent collection. Art lovers will find themselves getting lost as they wander for hours throughout the museum’s halls.

El Rosedal
thumbnails
calendar

2:30pm

As your weekend winds down, continue to enjoy the city’s expansive parks and green spaces as you head north toward Palermo along Avenida del Libertador. Once you hit the intersection with Avenida Sarmiento, turn in and visit the beautiful Rosedal rose garden tucked into the park locally known as “Los Lagos.” Do a few laps around the man-made lake while you watch the people whiz by on skateboards, bikes and rollerblades (you can also rent some if you’re up for it). When hunger strikes, fear not: all throughout the park you’ll find food carts, called carritos, serving up hot dogs and choripán and bondiola sandwiches. It’s some of the best food in town, and the price can’t be beat. Wash it down with a cold drink and then find your way underneath a palm tree for a nap in the shade. Hey, life is good.
