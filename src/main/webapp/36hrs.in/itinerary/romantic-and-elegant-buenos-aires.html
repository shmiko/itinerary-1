Romantic and Elegant Buenos Aires

Buenos Aires

 by Paige Nichols
$$
romantic

Visitors flock to Buenos Aires for its cosmopolitan culture, nightlife and lure of melancholy sensuality that characterizes its denizens, the porteños ('people of the port'Â). This energy, this magic, has been around since BA's beginnings, and is now hard-wired into every fiber of its being. Beyond the din of public transport and political demonstrations, the city oozes an effortless beauty and romance, making it the perfect setting for an intimate urban getaway. No matter the season, Buenos Aires is nothing short of enchanting.

Friday

Vasalissa Chocolatier
thumbnails
calendar

3:30pm

After a restorative nap, it's time to get walking. What better place to start than elegant Recoleta, home to the city's most famous (and monied) last names? Meander along exquisite Avenida Alvear, admiring the impressive architecture and upscale boutiques. Pull out all the stops and bestow a shiny bauble from family-owned Jean Pierre jewelers upon your beloved to start the holiday off right. Treat your sweet tooth and stop off at Vasalissa Chocolatier to pick up a box of handmade truffles, tucked into the most charming toile boxes and tied up with a bow. Head back to your home base, Algodón Mansion for a hot bath or a cat-nap before carrying on with your evening.

Argentines never rush, meaning you are welcome to take your time and savor every moment without a drop of guilt. Dining late is standard in Buenos Aires; you will also be pleased that no waiter will ever hurry you out by bringing the check unannounced. Book a table at A Nos Amours for a perfect blend of Parisian and Porteño romance in a quiet corner of Palermo. With its daily menu scrawled on a chalkboard and a vast organic wine list, you’ll be charmed by its rich simplicity. Order a heavenly risotto or grilled fish, split with a delicious bottle of Torrontés, the white grape unique to Argentina.

Milion Resto
thumbnails
calendar

0:00am

Before turning in, head for a nightcap at Milión. Situated in a refurbished French mansion, this restaurant-bar serves up some of the city’s best cocktails in an unbelievable setting. Cozy candlelit nooks and high ceilings lead to the grand dame – a white marble staircase cascading down to the lush garden from the back patio. Sip on a tart caipiroska and people-watch as you soak in the urban oasis, just steps from the city’s hustle and bustle.

Saturday

HG
thumbnails
calendar

12:30pm

If the best way to one’s heart is through the stomach, brunch at HG at the Fierro Hotel in Palermo Hollywood must be considered the high-speed expressway to get you there. Chef Hernán Gipponi turns out creative and playful dishes that will wow your palate, whether it’s a perfectly poached egg served with polenta and roasted mushrooms or a morsel of grilled skirt steak accompanied by a carrot-ginger puree and wheat berries. Ask for a table near the garden and prepare to truly fall in love with your food.

Blackmamba's
thumbnails
calendar

2:30pm

Walk off your multi-course meal with some window-shopping in the popular Palermo Soho neighborhood. Offering a blend of the country’s biggest brands and local boutique designers, it’s a charming area that fills up on the weekends. Start near the intersection of El Salvador and Armenia streets; then tap into your darker side with up and coming label BLACKMAMBA’s luxurious gothic leather goods and apparel. Other notable spots include Mishka for avant-garde shoes and Humawaca for to-die-for funky leather bags and accessories.

Four Seasons

5:30pm

After so much eating and shopping, it’s time for a different type of indulgence. Visit the Four Seasons Spa and book a relaxing couple’s aromatherapy massage. Close your eyes and spend the next few hours in total Nirvana.

Cocina Sunae
thumbnails
calendar

9:00pm

With its hidden streets and secretive allure, Buenos Aires is the ideal spot for a tryst. Its supper clubs – known as puertas cerradas – are perfect for romantic dinners as they offer a touch of privacy away from the crowds. Visit Cocina Sunae in Chacarita (exact address provided upon reservation) for authentic Southeast Asian flavors in an intimate setting. The menu changes weekly and features dishes such as spicy pork belly, Pad Thai, and red curry shrimp.

Sunday

Plaza Dorrego
thumbnails
calendar

10:30am

Before the masses flock to iconic Plaza Dorrego (Defensa and Humberto Primo streets) for the weekly fair, visit historic San Telmo early for a dose of colonial charm. Stroll the cobblestoned streets and get lost on its many side streets, stopping to admire the casa chorizo architecture style so typical of the area. Pop into its myriad of antique shops and rummage through the shelves in hopes of finding a real gem. Old seltzer bottles are a unique souvenir that speak to a time gone by, though they might weigh your suitcase down a bit with their lead taps and heavy glass bases. Just as the tourists come rushing in, head north to Recoleta for one last stop.

Museo Nacional de Arte Decorativo
thumbnails
calendar

12:30pm

Enjoy a quiet al fresco lunch at Croque Madame, which is nestled next to the marvelous Museum of Decorative Arts. From your table next to the bubbling fountain, order up a fresh salad or sandwich as you soak up the last rays of porteño sunshine. Save room for dessert and be sure to order the brownie and chocolate mousse cake, providing a very a sweet end to an even sweeter trip.
