Delhi for the Seekers

New Delhi

 by Naomi Hattaway
$
cultural
foodie

Delhi is a colorful city with the requirement that all senses be engaged, all of the time. From the fast-paced traffic to the non-stop honking and multi-faceted cultural happenings, sometimes a traveler needs to look inward. This itinerary is for explorers who are interested in a soulful experience, delving a bit deeper into the religion and culture of Delhi, with the added culinary delight of the best eateries in Delhi.

Friday

Nizammuddin Dargah
thumbnails
calendar

5:00pm

An early evening visit to the Nizamuddin Dargah (Mathura Road) is an enlightening and inspiring way to spend an hour or two. If you’re lucky, a qawwali will be performed during your visit. A qawwali is a form of Sufi devotional music that is beautiful singing accompanied by a harmonium and a percussion beat of some sort. Those participating in the qawwali will provide almost haunting music that you can find yourself lost in. If you can tear yourself away, make time to navigate the narrow alleyways to explore the rest of the area, including the mausoleum. Note: heads must be covered, and scarves can be purchased from vendors if you forget to bring your own. Also please be aware that women are not allowed inside the main shrine and shoes must be removed.

Bukhara
thumbnails
calendar

8:00pm

Make a reservation and ensure you’re hungry for this evening meal at Bukhara (ITC Maurya, Diplomatic Enclave, Sadar Patel Marg). The restaurant is located is a bit on the pricey side, but so well worth it. Think “heavy on the meat” but with quite a few equally powerful vegetarian options as well. Bukhara has won numerous awards and is touted as one of the best Indian cuisine restaurants in the world. Be forewarned, there is no cutlery and no children allowed below the age of 11.

Saturday

Hotel Sarvana Bhavan
thumbnails
calendar

9:00am

Rise and shine and head to Saravana Bhavan (P-15, Connaught Circus, near Madras Hotel), for a mouth-watering selection of dosas. The Saravana restaurant uses the more traditional spelling, dosai, which are large, crispy pancakes from South India. If you want a more laidback environment, hit up Haldirams (6 L-Block, Outer Circle, Connaught Circus) for similar fare.

Bangla Sahib Gurudwara
thumbnails
calendar

10:00am

Pack the same head covering from yesterday and make your way to the Gurudwara Bangla Sahib (Ashok Road near Connaught Place). Meander your way to the shoe check (they will exchange them for a token) and wash your hands and walk through the pool of water to cleanse your feet. To your right, take a stroll around the water tank, which is claimed to have healing powers.

The temple itself is open to people of all race and religion and taking a quiet seat near the back provides an interesting glance into the Sikh religion. When your stomach begins to growl, take the stairs to the left of the temple and you’ll soon discover the Langar Hall.

Over 500 people are typically served a free meal every 30 minutes beginning about noon. If you’re feeling curious, take a seat in the expansive hall and experience the humble act of communing with strangers.

Janpath market
thumbnails
calendar

12:00pm

Janpath, which means People’s Path, is noted as the most famous of markets in Delhi. However, you’ll be hard-pressed to feel crowded or packed in. You can find the most interesting of trinkets, souvenirs and collectible items. Don’t forget to make your list of who to buy for before you leave, and if you spot Pushpa and her row of second-hand saris, say hello. They make for incredible re-purposed home décor!

Moving towards peace and tranquility, the Lotus Temple (Kalkaji) is one of seven Bahá'í Houses of Worship. The laws of Bahá'í mandate that each structure have nine sides and the doors be open to all, regardless of religion. You’ll need to remove your shoes and be sure to take advantage of the carpet that is laid out so as not to burn your feet. Once inside, take a seat and meditate along with the chanting or even silence that you will find upon entering.

Karim's
thumbnails
calendar

7:00pm

After a full day on your feet, it’s time to take a load off and eat at the infamous Karim’s (Jama Masjid, Gali Kababian, Old Delhi). The tandoor is fired up and waiting for you to dive in to their extremely diverse and amazing menu.
