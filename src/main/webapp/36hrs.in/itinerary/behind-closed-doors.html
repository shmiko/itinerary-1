Behind Closed Doors

Buenos Aires

 by Allie Lazar
$$$
foodie
nightlife

Looking for the hottest eating and drinking tips in town? Well, shhhhhh you better be discreet because Buenos Aires is all about the underground dining and bar scene. Not so much a secret anymore, closed-door restaurants (known locally as puertas cerradas), have been all the rage for quite some time in this trendy porteño capital. Password protected bars, hidden trap door entrances, and (semi) top secret addresses, now you can join the cool kids and be in-the-know of BA's best underground spots.

Friday

Frank's
thumbnails
calendar

2:00am

With a few drinks under your belt, hop over to Palermo Hollywood for some high class cocktails at prohibition-era speakeasy Frank’s Bar. A password protected spot, upon entering with the correct password you’ll be given a numerical code to enter into a mock telephone booth, which will open into a long wooden corridor with adjacent erotic lingerie shop, and a sophisticated two-story bar. Watch as dapper catineros (mixologists) masterfully put on a show, whipping up wonderful alcoholic mixtures.

recoleta cemetery
thumbnails
calendar

4:00pm

The Recoleta Cemetery is still one of the top tourist destinations in Buenos Aires, where travelers can visit Argentina’s most famous leading lady, Eva Perón. Wander around the endless labyrinth-like paths of the city’s oldest cemetery visiting the tombs of presidents, military generals and artists. It may be flocking with foreigners, but it’s a site not to be missed.

Florencio Bistro & Patisserie
thumbnails
calendar

5:30pm

Since locals tend to eat lunch at midday, and dinner quite late (9pm – 11pm), merienda replaces breakfast as one of the most important meals of the day. You may need a GPS to map out how to get to Florencio nestled a few blocks away from the cemetery and just behind the British Embassy in a secluded island-like section of Recoleta. A true hidden gem, this tiny Italian bakery only has a handful of tables, but makes some of the best pastries in the city: medialunas, Nutella-chocolate cake and berry cheesecake. Order it with an Illy coffee and you’ll have a wonderful afternoon pick-me-up.

0-800-VINO
thumbnails
calendar

7:00pm

Learn about Argentine wine from expert Nigel Tollermann who hand selects some of the best local wines, and sells them at a great price. A leading internationally acclaimed sommelier, Nigel runs wine club 0800-Vino where he also hosts wine tastings in his Abasto wine cellar every Friday early evening.

A new bar and restaurant in Retiro, La Florería Atlántico is the creation of two of the city’s bar scene powerhouses. Don’t be too confused when you walk in to find a flower shop-wine store hybrid on the main floor, you will quickly be escorted to what is reminiscent of a heavy meatpacking refrigerator door that will lead you down to this nautical themed immigrant bar, with drinks influenced by the waves of Argentine immigration in the beginning of the 20th century (Italian, Spanish, Polish, French and English). The only bar in the city to make all the food on the parrilla (grill), Florería serves Italian and Spanish style tapas plates, and also some of the best beef in the city.

Saturday

878 Bar
thumbnails
calendar

0:00am

A pioneer in the world of underground bars, Ocho7Ocho is no stranger from making the top list of the World’s Best Bars where skilled bartenders concoct innovative cocktail creations. For the ultimate double closed-door experience, head to the way back where there is a second hidden bar and lounge. For those who want to keep the night going strong, head to house-converted into bar Ferona for some good drinks and even better eclectic music. Or local milonga (tango club) Salon Canning, where the average patron won’t be younger than 50 years old, but that all changes on the dance floor.

Oui Oui
thumbnails
calendar

10:30am

If you can manage to roll out of bed at a reasonable hour, head for breakfast at Palermo’s go-to brunch spot, Oui Oui. Coffee and warm gooey croissants are never a bad way to start your day, nor is the Tony breakfast special, which includes eggs Benedict, roasted potatoes and coffee.

El Rosedal
thumbnails
calendar

11:30am

Walk off lunch by visiting one of Palermo’s many parks. A place where local families and exercisers congregate on the weekends, rent bikes or go on a paddle boat ride in the middle of the rose garden, El Rosedal (Av Infanta Isabel & Iraola).

Foto Ruta
thumbnails
calendar

2:00pm

A new concept in city tours, Foto Ruta lets travelers connect with the city in their own way on this photography scavenger hunt. Each week the unguided urban tour ventures off to discover a different neighborhood, with just a list of clues and a camera (professional or smart phone) in hand.

I Latina
thumbnails
calendar

9:00pm

The Colombian Macías brothers at I Latina, who have been banging out modern and inventive Caribbean dishes out of their secret Villa Crespo location, quickly became one of the most successful closed-door restaurants in the city. Resembling a private reservation-only restaurant, each night the kitchen prepares an exquisite five-course tasting menu with wine pairings. Think dishes like braised cane sugar pork shoulder with Andean potatoes or a salmon tartare with a quail egg and plantain chip.

Sunday

Plaza Dorrego
thumbnails
calendar

10:30am

Head to San Telmo for the legendary Sunday antique market and fair. Walk down Defensa street to Plaza Dorrego for souvenir shopping and antique scavenging.

El rey del chori
thumbnails
calendar

12:00pm

Keep your eyes peeled for smoke-filled air, and follow your nose down Defensa Street for a hidden meat-garden haven. Serving some of the best choripán in the city only on Sundays, El Rey del Chori (Mexico and Defensa) consists of mega-grills overflowing with Argentina’s most famous fast food. It’s a crime to visit Buenos Aires without tasting the famous helado so afterwards walk down to try the famous Patagonian sweet stuff at Abuela Goye (Defensa and Carlos Calvo), with artisanal flavors like Double Dulce de Leche and Dark Chocolate.
