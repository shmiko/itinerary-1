Living the High Life

Mendoza

 by Amanda Barnes
$$$
luxury
wine

Mendoza is characterized by cowboys, vineyards and mountains; but aside from its great rustic charm, it also offers some of life’s ultimate luxuries. As a province that has boomed with international tourism over the last 10 years, there are no limits to the new heights of luxury travel available. Take your time to indulge in gourmet experiences, space out in spa relaxation and enjoy the privilege of the unblemished, idyllic outdoors.

Wine Lovers

Friday

Grapevine Wine Tours
thumbnails
calendar

5:00pm

Take to the sky to really see what Mendoza is all about. An hour long private flight will show you how this desert sprang into life from snowmelt and sparkling blue dams which channel glacial water to irrigate the blanket spread of vineyards that drape down from the foothills of the Andes. Finish off with a glass of sparkling wine as you touchdown at sunset and watch the tapestry of mountain colors change. Book a flight through custom tour company Grapevine Wine Tours.

1884 Francis Mallmann
thumbnails
calendar

9:30pm

The ultimate Argentine chef, Francis Mallmann, is still considered one of South America’s finest and after more than 30 years at the top, who’s to disagree? Mendoza is home to two of his restaurants: 1884 and 7 Fuegos. The acclaimed 1884 restaurant is tucked in a winery just outside the city center where a grandiose setting gives way to even more grandiose food and a wine list longer than Homer’s Odyssey. For a more outdoors experience, make your way to his new 7 Fuegos restaurant in The Vines of Mendoza vineyard property in Uco Valley, 1.5 hours south of the city. Seven courses of different Argentine BBQ techniques paired with great wines beneath a canopy of stars. Both offer ample opportunity to savor Argentina’s world-renowned steak.

Saturday

Pulenta Estate
thumbnails
calendar

9:30am

The early bird catches the worm and in Mendoza that bird drinks world-class Malbec. Organise a private chauffeur from your hotel to drive you out to Pulenta Estate in Lujan de Cuyo, 1 hour from the city. Brothers Eduardo and Hugo Pulenta, who have one of the richest family histories in Argentine wine, created the winery to make premium wines. A private tour includes an aroma session and a tasting of the icon wines including their fabulous Cabernet Franc. If you want true indulgence, ask for a paired chocolate tasting – nothing quite as epicurean as chocolate and silky red wine for breakfast!

Uco Valley
thumbnails
calendar

11:00am

Lujan de Cuyo is still the ‘home’ of Malbec, but the new Uco Valley wine region is Mendoza’s wildest and also most stunning. Uninterrupted mountain views with fields and vines as far as the eye can see, a slice of heaven and home to the most modern wineries and luxury accommodation options.

Bodega O'Fournier
thumbnails
calendar

12:30pm

Owned by Spanish wine legend Jose Ortega, the state-of-the-art winery O-Fournier has striking, other worldly architecture set next to the imposing mountains. Take a private tour through the winery and the owner’s private art collection and finish in the minimalist restaurant with outstanding landscape views and an award-winning menu of six courses with wine pairing. This is one of the ultimate experiences of Mendoza’s new investments and world-class architecture.

Estancia San Pablo
thumbnails
calendar

4:00pm

As the intrepid, discerning traveller knows, ultimate luxury doesn’t just mean comfort – it is about exclusivity. The authentic horse riding experience in Estancia Puablo, one of the most privileged private estates in Mendoza, is pure, private luxury with handsome horses, two hours of vigorous riding isolated in the foothills of the Andes and guided by rugged – but gentlemanly – gauchos (Argentine cowboys). Finish your afternoon off with a glass of their limited production wine in the traditional cellar with the owner and some of their home-cured Jamon Serrano. Alternatively you can do fly fishing or outdoor mountain activities.

Pirca Cerveza Artesanal
thumbnails
calendar

9:30pm

It’s all about closed-door restaurants in Mendoza. The city’s best chefs and most eccentric characters all have their private dining options where you are invited to their house to sample course on course of exquisite morsels of Argentine and South American fusion cuisine while drinking some of the best wine on offer and learning about Argentine culture and gastronomy from the experts. Try Pirca Food & Wine, Ituzaingo, Los Chocos or hire Pablo del Rio or Nadia Heron to come and cook for you.

Sunday

Cacheuta
thumbnails
calendar

11:00am

The beauty of Mendoza is that it is still a land of times past. Sundays are still Sundays and it is all about spending time with your family and relaxing to the full. There's no more Andean way to chill out than tucked away in the middle of the mountains soothing your bones in the steaming waters of Cacheuta springs. For the lux end, pick the Hotel and Spa resort where you have a full buffet lunch and can book in for a Swedish massage. If your budget is a little tight, the waterpark next door has springs but they are more family oriented. Finish off your stay in Mendoza soaking up the sun and springs while looking onto the rugged splendor of the Andes. Alternatively if you want to hide away for a few hours visit Entre Cielos hammam, 20 mins from the city. A modern spa where you do a hammam circuit in complete privacy and can finish with wine treatments and foam massage.
