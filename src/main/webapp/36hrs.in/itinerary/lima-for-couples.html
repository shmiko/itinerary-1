Lima for Couples

Lima

 by Walter Rhein
$$$
romantic
sightseeing
luxury

Between the fine dining, the dancing shows, and the beautiful water parks, Lima can be a great city to explore with that special someone. You're virtually guaranteed a pleasant evening as the weather in Lima is mild and perfect for an evening stroll. This itinerary will provide you with a nice mix of romantic excursions to make sure your stay over in Lima is a memorable one.

Friday

Parque de la Reserva
thumbnails
calendar

6:00pm

Lima's water park is the jewel of the city and is available to you for a trifling S/. 4 fee (less than $2). You can easily spend a couple hours wandering around the beautiful fountains either during the day, or at night when the water is lit up in a spectacular laser light show. Alternatively, you could do this tour in conjunction with Lima's Mirabus, a double-decker bus tour of the city which starts in Miraflores at 6 PM. The guided tour offers you a great look at the water park, as well as some other notable landmarks of the city for only S/. 65 (about $25).

Panchita
thumbnails
calendar

10:00pm

Another of Gaston Acurio's creations, Panchita is an excellent choice for an evening meal. The atmosphere is elegant and refined, and the food is simply spectacular. Panchita specializes in traditional Peruvian anticuchos (meat served on a skewer), but the menu offers items for a wide variety of tastes. Panchita also offers a fine selection of Pisco drinks which are sure to get the evening rolling. You might also wish to sample what is widely regarded as one of Lima's best sangrias.

Saturday

Paragliding
thumbnails
calendar

9:00am

After enjoying a light, continental breakfast at your hotel, you can head down to the Costa Verde for a morning of paragliding. Coupled with a pilot, you will be able to take a tandem ride along the updrafts of the Costa Verde and be afforded the chance to take some photos your friends aren't going to believe. There are several schools along the coast that offer Paragliding tours that usually cost around S/. 100 ($35) for a 45 minute flight.

Larcomar
thumbnails
calendar

12:00pm

After paragliding, you can walk down the Costa Verde, through the lovely “Parque de Amor” on your way to Larco Mar. Larco Mar is the local shopping area, and there are a wide variety of fine restaurants there to choose from. Larco Mar is built right into the coast, so all of the ocean-facing establishments offer spectacular views. The best view is probably provided by Mangos, but any of the gourmet restaurants on the first level are good choices. Expect to pay around $25 for your meal. When you're finished eating, save some room for desert and grab an ice-cream cone at Laritza D near the food court.

Las Catacumbas
thumbnails
calendar

2:00pm

After lunch, you can take a taxi up Avenida Arequipa to the Center of Lima. This is Lima's historical district, and explore the many beautiful cathedrals, plazas, and general architecture. You can start with the Catacumbas de San Francisco. The tombs beneath the structure are said to hold the remains of over 70,000 people who died when Peru was ravaged by plague. The entry fee is S/. 7 ($2.5)

Plaza de Armas
thumbnails
calendar

4:00pm

After the Catacombs, you can take a five minute walk to the Plaza de Armas. The Plaza de Armas is Lima's central square and contains the Palacio de Gobierno as well as the Lima Cathedral. It is definitely worth taking the tour of the Lima Cathedral which houses the remains of Lima's founder Francisco Pizarro.

Jiron de la union
thumbnails
calendar

6:00pm

Once you have finished the Plaza de Armas, you can walk down Jiron de la Union. This is a foot-traffic only street which is a hustling, bustling marketplace where virtually everything can be obtained, and every price is negotiable. This is a great place to purchase some mementos or gifts for your friends and family back at home. A word of warning, although this is not a dangerous street the presence of crowds is the type of cover that is enticing to pick-pockets. Many tourists are prone to swinging their backpacks around to their chests for additional security. However, if you stay alert and keep an eye on your partner, you won't encounter any problems. The Jiron de La Union is also a great place to find a quick snack if you're getting hungry.

Another of Lima's Historical squares, the Plaza San Martín is a fine place to finish off your walking tour, or sit and relax at a bar or cafe.

Brisas Del Titicaca
thumbnails
calendar

9:15pm

Las Brisas del Titicaca will be the major event of your evening. This is rated as Lima's best folklore dance show. Food and drink is available as you sit and enjoy the spectacle of various demonstrations of Peru's traditional dance. An intimate seating arrangement puts you right next to the show, and between numbers, the audience is encouraged to join the dance floor. The party at Brisas del Titicaca lasts long after the last scheduled dance and it's one of Lima's better parties. Call early for a reservation. Prices are around S/. 60 (about $20).
