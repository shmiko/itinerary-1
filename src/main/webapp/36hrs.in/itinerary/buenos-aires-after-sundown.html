Buenos Aires after Sundown

Buenos Aires

 by Paige Nichols
$$
nightlife

A large part of the magic of Buenos Aires is that it truly is a city that never sleeps. Dinners that start at midnight and late-night parties that last until well beyond sunrise mean that getting some shuteye takes a backseat in the porteño list of priorities. If you've come to BA for a good time, it won't be hard to fill the agenda with a variety of parties and social events. The best part? New spots are always opening up, so check with the locals to find out where the party du jour will be popping up.

Buenos Aires on a Shoestring

Thursday

Gardiner
thumbnails
calendar

10:30pm

It seems as though the weekend never ends in BA, with the dedicated club kids pulling all-nighters even on Sundays, but Thursday is a top night to hit the dance floor. Before you sip on some champagne or the local favorite caipiroska cocktail, stop for some dinner. Gardiner (Costanera Norte y La Pampa) is located on the river in a part of town known as the Costanera, and provides more than enough eye candy to accompany your meal. From the jets soaring over the lawn (local airport Aeroparque is just across the way) to the long line of local celebrities – TV mogul Marcelo Tinelli and famous soccer coach Coco Basile, among others – eating here is often quite the experience. Order a delicious piece of grilled salmon or a succulent cut of steak and wash it down with a great bottle of wine before you put your dancing shoes on.

Tequila
thumbnails
calendar

0:30am

If you were wondering why Gardiner was so packed, it’s because exclusive nightclub Tequila is just steps away. Tequila (Costanera Norte y La Pampa) is the place to see and be seen, that is, if you can get past the velvet rope. Its notoriously tough door policy is known for favoring the glitterati, but pulling the 'tourist from out of town' card can often work to your advantage. Make sure you dress the part – the dance floor often feels more like a runway at times. Order a cocktail, soak in the scene and get ready to dance until the sun rises over the River Plate.

Sleep in – you’ll need your stamina for the rest of the weekend – and recharge your batteries with an indulgent brunch. Local favorite Café Crespin in Villa Crespo serves up delicious cinnamon rolls, fluffy scrambled eggs, crispy bacon and more heavenly treats. Do as the locals and order an extra large café con leche; caffeine courses through the veins of the porteños on a constant basis. Spend your afternoon lazying about, taking a stroll down through Palermo Viejo to burn off last night’s excesses and be sure to sneak in another catnap before taking on the next great fiesta.

Campobravo
thumbnails
calendar

10:00pm

Partake in another unhurried dinner with friends before embarking on a bar crawl of the city’s hottest watering holes. A parrilla like Campo Bravo infuses the festive atmosphere throughout the restaurant, and it seems as though diners are getting a head-start on checking out whom they might invite for a drink later on. If you’ve tired of steak, indulge at Peruvian-Japanese fusion restaurant Osaka for some incredible ceviches and creative cocktails.

Ferona
thumbnails
calendar

0:00am

With so many options on the nightlife scene, why commit to just one place for the evening? Hit the pavement and visit some of the newest favorites taking over. Those looking for an intimate house party feel should head to Ferona Social Club, a homegrown bar with a European air, sitting pretty in some very cool digs in Palermo Hollywood. There’s no sign outside, but ring the bell with confidence and you’ll be fine. The rooftop terrace gets more packed as the night goes on, but the downstairs living room provides plenty of space to curl up with a cocktail and some amazing tunes.

Frank's
thumbnails
calendar

1:45am

After you’ve hit your stride, keep your buzz going with a stopover at Frank’s, another speakeasy that promises a roaring time behind closed doors. To gain access, you’ll need to decipher the password by visiting the Facebook back and gathering the clues. Once you’re in, Frank’s serves up classic cocktails given a new twist, and the people watching is always a treat – you’ll wonder who might be a famous model or local musician (or who just dresses like they were). Pay your tab and get ready for one last stop … the night is still in diapers, as locals say!

Unicorn Huset
thumbnails
calendar

3:00am

If you’re craving a bit of dancing, fear not. Move on to Unicorn Huset, a mix of rock-n’-roll and chic that promises something for everyone. Those needing a bit of a rest can head upstairs, while the party animals can head straight to the dance floor. You’ll probably end up looking a little worse for the wear after weaving your way through the heaving mass of partygoers. When you’re ready to call it a night, hail a cab and head home – or you can visit local classic Kentucky (Av. Santa Fe y Godoy Cruz), a 24-hour pizza joint that always tastes better after a long night out.

Saturday

Jet Lounge Club
thumbnails
calendar

0:00am

Let’s be honest, you spent the entire day in bed and barely managed to pick up the phone to order empanada delivery. That’s OK – only the seasoned clubgoers can keep up a pace like this and function normally during the day. On your last night, go out with a bang and make sure you visit one more boliche (nightclub) before you leave town. Jet Lounge Club – located along the same stretch as Tequila – is the perfect spot to seal your trip to BA. Strap on your tallest heels or button up your finest shirt and get ready to make another good impression to the bouncers. DJs serve up the hottest mix of electronica and the bar is always packed with orders for beverage of choice champagne. If you can last until dawn, your reward will be the panoramic views of the sunrise over the local marina. It’s definitely worth the effort.

Costanera Carrito's
thumbnails
calendar

5:30am

Hungry? Before hopping into a taxi, stop off at one of the carritos along the Costanera, always open and always serving up some delicious street meat. Order a massive sandwich de bondiola, grilled juicy pork topped with fresh chimichurri and salsa criolla. The mix of local fishermen up early to start their days and clubgoers stumbling home after a marathon night out make for quite the surreal experience. As you drive off, remember that next time you’re in town, there will be a new crop of bars and clubs to try, and new dance floors to break in.
