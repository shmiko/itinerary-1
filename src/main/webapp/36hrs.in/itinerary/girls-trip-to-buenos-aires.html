Girls' Trip to Buenos Aires

Buenos Aires

 by Paige Nichols
$$
shopping

Even though Buenos Aires makes quite a hard sell when it comes to sultry sexiness and romance, it's actually a great city to enjoy with a group of close friends. Unexpected activities, trendsetting restaurants, and hip shopping districts make it an ideal place for a girls' trip. Besides, wine and ice cream are more fun when shared, right?

Friday

Creme de la Creme
thumbnails
calendar

3:00pm

The past few years have given rise to a new wave of fresh local designers turning out avant-garde and utterly fabulous pieces that add sparkle to any wardrobe. Save yourself a bit of the legwork of navigating the scene and book a bespoke tour with fashion guru Vanessa from Crème de la Crème (by appointment). She will guide you through the best of BA’s hip boutiques and provide access to showrooms not typically available for public access. Take your time throughout the afternoon, and head back to your hotel for a mini fashion-show and nap before your evening begins.

Sipan Cevicheria Peruana
thumbnails
calendar

9:30pm

The Palermo Soho neighborhood still remains one of the more chic and happening spots to head for a bite to eat on a Friday night. Bright young things strut their stuff with all they’ve got, and it seems as though everyone is posing for cameras that aren’t actually there. Soak up this atmosphere from a privileged table at Sipan, a Peruvian-Japanese fusion restaurant located in the Palermitano Hotel that serves up incredible ceviches, sushi and cocktails. Dishes are made to share, so the only trouble you’ll have will be choosing which ones to order – they’re all mouth wateringly good.

Isabel B. de Ferari
thumbnails
calendar

0:00am

After dinner, pop next door to Isabel to mingle among the city’s beautiful people, drink in hand. The space is lined with opulent boots along one wall, and a magnificent bar along the other. Bartenders serve up potent potables such as the Suffering Bastard, a modern twist on the mint julep, and more traditional “Golden Age” cocktails. DJ’s spin tunes that will get you dancing, though when you need a breath of fresh air, head out to the back patio -complete with fire pit- for a break. Try not to get turned around in the all-mirrored unisex bathrooms.

Enjoy a lazy breakfast in Villa Crespo, one of BA’s coolest barrios just steps beyond Palermo. Café Crespin serves delicious deli-style bites such as heavenly French toast, fresh-baked bagels with lox, and to-die-for fruit smoothies (the passion fruit is a favorite). Don’t be deterred by the long lines and crowds: it’s definitely worth the fuss. Gather up energy for your afternoon activity; you’ll need it as you pound the pavement.

Foto Ruta
thumbnails
calendar

2:00pm

Let’s be honest, no one wants to be 'that tourist' – bulky camera strap around your neck, large folding map unfurling in the wind, hideously 'practical' walking shoes. No, the best way to see a city is through an unconventional, more adventurous lens. Join in for an afternoon with Foto-Ruta - its weekly tours are an excellent way to roam the city with more independence and creativity. After meeting in a local neighborhood café, you’ll learn some street photography tips and tricks. Before you know it, you’re handed a list of 'clues' germane to the barrio and are given a few hours to set out and photograph each clue as you interpret it. The group then reconvenes over a glass of wine to review the day’s snaps. You’ll leave not only with some great memories, but also with an arsenal of photo tips that anyone from novice to professional can appreciate.

For another dose of BA’s nightlife, head underground – literally. Florería Altántico is part flower & wine shop, part speakeasy restaurant, the brainchild of those behind both uber-successful 878 and the Fierro Hotel. A hidden refrigerator door on the ground floor leads down a narrow stairwell to an effortlessly cool and completely unpretentious space. It was designed to be reminiscent of the bars European immigrants visited upon arriving to the city’s port; mythical serpentine creatures dance across the walls and a steady soundtrack of jazz and other classic tunes filters through the speakers. It’s not a large place, so be sure to call ahead and book a table. Order any of the inventive cocktails whipped up by celebrity bartender Tato Giovannoni - such as the Transatlántico Fizz or the Ginebra y Tónica with a sprig of thyme – before tucking in to delicacies such as a grilled octopus tentacle or whole fish served with fresh green beans and an orange glaze.

Head back to Palermo Soho for one last lunch and shopping loop before your trip comes to a close. Sit pretty on the rooftop terrace at Quimbombo, which serves up natural food with an exotic twist. If you're hungry, the Indian thali platter is a home run; just be sure to order a large Ginger Rosado (pink grapefruit, ginger, mint and lots of crushed ice) to wash everything down. From here you'll have a great view of the neighborhood, spotting the shops you missed on Friday and remembering that pair of shoes you were dying to try on...
