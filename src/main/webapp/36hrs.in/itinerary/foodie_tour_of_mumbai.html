Foodie Tour of Mumbai

Mumbai

 by bombayjules
$$
foodie

Mumbai is without doubt, the most cosmopolitan city in India - but it is also the most itinerant.  Over many decades, millions of people have flocked to the city from rural villages all over India, each one bringing their own traditions, faiths and recipes.  Nowhere else in India will you find such a variety of regional cooking; rich meat curries from the Punjab; coconutty prawn curries from Kerala; vegetarian thalis from Gujurat; Irani influenced Parsi berry pulavs; the Portuguese balchaos of Goa.  More recently, European restaurants have been making a foray on to the Mumbai restaurant scene and there is some top-notch western cooking to be found in the city. This is what makes Mumbai such Foodie Heaven.

The Best Rooftop Bars

Friday

Cafe Britannia
thumbnails
calendar

3:00pm

After settling in at your hotel, make your way over to Britannia in Ballard Estate in Fort for a late lunch.  Britannia is Mumbai’s un-missable, best-loved restaurant and one of the few remaining Parsi dining rooms.  Food-wise, the eatery is most noted for its chicken berry pulav (containing barberries imported from Iran) and sali boti (lamb curry topped with fried potato straws). But the most famous aspect of this restaurant is its owner – the charismatic Boman Kohinoor.  Boman is an avid Anglophile and fan of the British Royal Family and if he spots an English person he will go over and show them his collection of Royal memorabilia.  Very eccentric! The restaurant is open 11.30am – 4pm and is not open on Sundays (and don’t forget to try out every Parsi’s favourite soft drink – Pallonji’s raspberry soda).

The Tea Center
thumbnails
calendar

4:30pm

Staying slightly with the British Colonial theme, catch a taxi to The Tea Centre in Churchgate (78 Veer Nariman Road). At this heritage tea lounge, you can sample some of India’s premium teas (such as the ‘Maharani of Darjeeling’ or ‘Good Old Masala Chai) as well as partake in some snacks.  If you are not full up after your chicken berry pulav, try their cheese-on-toast or perhaps one of the ‘Tea Fancies’ to satiate a sweet tooth (muffins, scones, applie pie etc).

The Dome
thumbnails
calendar

7:00pm

Freshen up at your hotel, head over to the Intercontinental hotel on Marine Drive and up to the top floor where you will find the swanky Dome Bar.  This is the best place for a sun-downer drink in Mumbai – with views stretching up Marine Drive and across the Arabian Sea.  Relax on one of the bar’s large white sofas with a white wine spritzer or a Kingfisher beer (or indeed sample one the many cocktails on offer) and enjoy the muted sounds of ambient house music. Later, snack on succulent kebabs brought up from the hotel’s Indian restaurant ‘Kebab Corner’ – but don’t eat too much as tomorrow is going to be a heavy day! (Note: During the monsoon period June to September, there is a temporary cover on the Dome Bar – but you can still get good views).

Saturday

Reality Tours & Travel
thumbnails
calendar

11:00am

Mumbai’s top hotels all do fantastic brunches (with or without alcohol), which are a great way to spend time with friends and unwind.  However, today, please enjoy breakfast at your hotel but keep it light - as the afternoon is going to be spent eating! 

Head over to the location of your pre-arranged ‘Food Walk’.  Food Walks are very popular in Mumbai right now, and are the perfect way to experience the cuisines of local communities and street food in a safe environment.  Kalyan Kamarkar of Finely Chopped leads the most well organised and informative of these walks.  The locations for these forays can vary – for example: Fort, Bandra, Bohri Mohalla, Oshiwara and Matunga. All of these neighbourhoods offer contrasting cuisines – for example Muslim meat based cuisine in Bohri Mahalla; Bengal (or ‘Bong’ food) in Oshiwara; the spicy South Indian food of Matunga; the Parsi outlets of Fort.  Notice for these walks is usually given one or two weeks before – so please keep an eye on the Finely Chopped website ahead of your trip.  Not only do these Food Walks give you the chance to sample all types of Indian cuisines, but you will also get to see the ‘real’ side of the city. Tours are usually around 2,000 Rs per head and will include four food stops and a few breaks to do ingredient/souvenir shopping (spices, coffee, bread etc). Private bespoke walks can be arranged with Kalyan from 8,000 Rs. Email him at k.finelychopped@gmail.com

Alternatively you can organise yourself a Reality Tours Street Food Tour, which can also be arranged ahead. Through this tour, you will discover authentic street foods such as Mumbai’s beloved bhel puri, vada pav and pav bhaji – all in a safe and hygienic environment.  The price is 1,300 Rs per head for a group of up to six.  Note – this tour starts at 5.30 pm until 9pm so can be swapped with the evening option below.

Sea Lounge, Taj Mahal Palace Hotel
thumbnails
calendar

4:00pm

You will be extremely full by now but you might fancy a cup of tea.  Experience the grandeur of colonial Bombay by heading over to the Taj Palace Hotel on Apollo Bunder (checking out the Gateway of India first).  Walk up the stairs to the Sea Lounge on the first floor and order yourself a cup of Silver Grove White Tips Darjeeling Tea. Smooth as a silk sari. Sit back, relax and look out to sea.

Mahesh Lunch Home
thumbnails
calendar

8:00pm

After a day of solid eating and drinking, you may want to give this option a miss.  But if you still have a bit of space left, get in your car/taxi and make your way to Mahesh Lunch Home (also open for dinner) in at 8B, Cawasji Patel Street, Fort. Time to sample some of the best Mangalorean seafood available in the city.  Such dishes as the sumptuous Prawn Gassi or crispy Bombay Duck Fry are an absolute must. (Note: The famous Bombay Duck is actually a fish).  Accompany your meal with a lassi, salty Sol Kadi or a fresh lime soda.

blueFROG
thumbnails
calendar

10:00pm

If you still have the energy, it’s now time to experience cool-central at Mumbai’s finest music club – the Blue Frog.  You don’t have to dine if you don’t fancy it – but if you do want a snack, there’s plenty to choose from their global menu. Of more interest is their extensive drinks menu and creative cocktail list.  Check out the website for details of upcoming gigs – which cover a wide range of genres. 

As an alternative – experience the New York vibe of Café Zoe nearby to the Blue Frog. With its stripped-brick warehouse interior you will find it a great place to sample a few cocktails and enjoy a non-Indian menu (perhaps a burger if you skipped dinner)?

Round off your culinary tour with a traditional South Indian breakfast at Café Madras - one of Matunga’s famed South Indian restaurants. Try food items such as such as idlis (rice dumplings), dosas (rice batter pancakes), vadas (spicy potato patties) and rassam (a South Indian soup).  Of course, you should accompany this with a fantastic, milky South Indian coffee, which will be poured, from a height into your cup to aerate it.  Café Madras can get very busy at the weekends so you may find a queue.
