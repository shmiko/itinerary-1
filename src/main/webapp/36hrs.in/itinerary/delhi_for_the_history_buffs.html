Delhi for the History Buffs

New Delhi

 by Naomi Hattaway
$
cultural
sightseeing

Established in 736 AD, the city of Delhi has a long history of empires and dynasties. The 5,000 years that the city has been existence gives it a ranking of one of the oldest inhabited cities in the world! You could spend days upon days learning about the deep history of Delhi, but this jam-packed itinerary hits the highlights of this amazing city and puts you in the prime locations to soak in the facts and relish in the tastes of India.

Friday

Jantar Mantar
thumbnails
calendar

5:00pm

Begin your weekend at Jantar Mantar, built in the early 1700s and one of the world’s oldest astronomical observatories. The bizarre orange-pink sculptures serve more than as just an artistic collection, but serve as predictors of the movement of the sun, moon and planets. Jantar means instrument and Mantar means formula. A favorite is the Ram Yantra with all of its planks and angles.

Journey to the Garden of Five Senses for a dinner at Magique (reservations strongly required, +91 9717535533). The restaurant is set inside a colonial-style house with a garden that also serves as a delightful seating area, lit with twinkling lights and filled with amazing ambience.

Don’t worry if you don’t have enough time in your schedule to make the trek to Agra for the Taj Mahal. Humayun’s Tomb is a gorgeous structure set on beautiful gardens. Built in the 1500s, the Char Bagh Gardens house the tomb, with water channels that intersect with each other all around the property and divide the gardens into 36 equal squares. Visit just after sunrise for a spectacular walk on the premises.

Qutub Minar
thumbnails
calendar

10:00am

Standing at nearly 238 feet tall, the entire Qutb Minar tower was hand carved, with the text from the Quarn on almost every surface. If it’s hard to imagine how tall this tower is, if the Great Sphinx of Giza in Egypt was stood upright, it would be as tall as the Qutb.

Make your way next to Gandhi Smriti (Birla House) for an absolutely enlightening morning. Allow at least two hours to fully explore the museum and the house where Mahatma Gandhi lived for the 144 days before his death.

The Spice Route
thumbnails
calendar

1:00pm

Take a load off and indulge your taste buds to lunch at Spice Route, inside The Imperial, a gorgeous hotel. The décor features art and culture reflecting the route of spices from Kerala though Sri Lanka, Malaysia, Indonesia and Thailand. The hand-painted murals will amaze you, as they are all hand-painted and the menu also does not disappoint. Request the pagoda for an extra-special experience.

Dilli Haat
thumbnails
calendar

2:00pm

Ready for some shopping and cultural experience? Visit Dilli Haat for the best souvenir shopping, with over 60 stalls containing handmade toys and soaps, jewelry, leather goods and even chappals. Practice saying “kitnaa” which is Hindi for ‘how much’ and be prepared to argue for the best price. Many afternoons will find performers and you can find vendors to decorate your arms or ankles with mehendi as well.

India Gate
thumbnails
calendar

4:00pm

Start to wind down your day by enjoying the festive atmosphere of India Gate in the late afternoon. Built in honor of the soldiers who fought in World War I and the Afghan Wars, there is an eternal flame that was lit in 1971 and still burns today.  The India Gate park off of Rajpath becomes filled with families picnicking and paddle boating, with small trinkets and food for sale as well.

The Red Fort is the largest monument in Old Delhi and it’s great expansiveness can be a bit overwhelming. Start first though by strolling through the Chatta Chowk (covered bazaar) still used today as a space for selling trinkets, scarves and other goodies. The interior of the Red Fort covers over 254 acres of land and you may need to prioritize your visit to see either the mosque, halls for public addresses, private apartments or the Royal Baths. Time your visit to also participate in the sound and light show, offered in both Hindi and English.

Sunday

Purana Qila
thumbnails
calendar

8:00am

Another early morning finds you at the bottom of the hill leading up to the entrance of Purana Qila (Old Fort). Built on top of a mound which is said to contain the ruins of the city of Indraprastha, the walls are almost 60 feet tall. Although there are three gates, one was closed reportedly, after a king left for battle and refused to come home until the enemy was conquered. He never returned, and the gate remains closed.
