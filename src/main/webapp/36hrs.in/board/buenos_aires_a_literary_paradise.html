Acasares

Maipu 994

Although there are plaques dotted all over the city locating Borges' various abodes during his life in the city, the apartment he spent the longest in is actually at 6B at Maipu 994, just off Plaza San Martin. You can't see much but this was his street for 40 years making it apt for roaming and hypothesising. La Cuidad bookstore in the shopping gallery in front of his apartment still has photos of him in that very bookstore and also find a couple first editions of his in the window.

El Ateneo Grand Splendid

Fight the hunger pangs like a true writer and head up from Plaza San Martin onto Avenida Santa Fe to El Ateneo - a stunning old theatre now completely dedicated to our paperback friends. Browse the enormous collection and pick a selection to take back downstairs to muse over while drinking a coffee on the old stage.

Plazoleta Julio CortÃ¡zar (Plaza Serrano)

Take the metro, cab or walk up to Plaza Cortazar (Serrano) in the heart of Palermo. This is one of the city's trendiest spots and hosts weekend artisan markets but its name still reflects its rich literary history. Originally named Serrano it was renamed Cortazar after the famed Argentine author, but then has lapsed back into being Plaza Serrano in ode to the times when Borges lived here. Grab a bite at one of the many bars and take time to people watch and get inspired for new characters in your novel.

Dain Usina Cultural

A bookshop-cum-cultural space-cum-bar-cum-restaurant-cum-live music venue, Dain Usina may have all the flippancy of a character in a telenovela, but this attractive space certainly gives you a culture fix. Browse the beautiful book collection, catch a book launch or poetry reading, check out some live music or just sip cocktails on the terrace.

To finish off your literary weekend you have to make it to "the point where all points meet" in the fictional world of Borges as depicted in The Aleph.
