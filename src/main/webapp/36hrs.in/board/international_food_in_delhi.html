International food in Delhi

New Delhi

 by Ravijot Chugh
foodie

Delhi restaurant circuit is not just Kebabs and Paranthas. These 10 outstanding restaurants bring different and delicious flavors of the world to Delhi. Be ready to splash the cash!

3

Neung Roi

Neung Roi is the reference for Thai food in Delhi, and dare I say, in India. The menu features meal options from different regions of Thailand, each carefully explained. It is possible to have an entire meal of different courses from one region, or if you are feeling adventurous, mix and match according to your will.

4

The Big Chill Cafe

Not the most authentic Italian food by any stretch of the imagination, but Big Chill has carved a spectacular niche for itself in all these years. An electric ambiance, consistent taste and not over the top prices have made it rate high on everyone's list. The Fusili Chicken Piri Piri pasta and Belgian Chocolate shake are must haves.

Orient Express

A classic that has been part of 'Best of Delhi' lists for almost 3 decades now, Orient Express is probably the most romantic restaurant in the city.Â A replica of a Pullman Orient Express carriage, there is a touch of class to everything that is done here. Dishing out a modern European fare, the restaurant also offers an extensive wine list, a great rare whiskey collection and masterful cocktails.Â

6

Sevilla

Sevilla is not a restaurant you come across everyday in Delhi. Its got a beautiful tent setting, candle light, live music, and a large open kitchen which steals the show. The paella, pato (smoked duck breast) and the wild mushrooms are must haves.

West View

West view has one of the best and most popular Sunday brunches in the city. Has a live salad bar for you to chose from and for the main course, you pick your choice of veggies or meat and hand them to the chef to have it grilled. The grill offers an extensive selection from lobsters to lamb and pork chops, salmon, and different cuts of beef.Â

Olive Bar & Kitchen

The Olive Bar and Kitchen is a rustic hideaway where good food, culture and conversation come together in a delightful Mediterranean melting pot. With an amazing location with the Qutab Minar towering above, it's hard to go wrong. The thin crust pizza, the Cod and house special are some of the tasty goodies you must try.

10

The House of Ming

The decor here is far from that of a usual oriental restaurant. As expected from the Taj, the service is exemplary. Whatever you do, do not miss the duck pancakes or one of the many dimsums. The spinach dumpling and the Barbecue mushroom are delicious offerings for the vegetarians.
