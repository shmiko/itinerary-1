The City of Kings

Lima

Over the last decade Lima has transformed itself from a checkpoint on the way to Machu Picchu into a gastronomic wonderland and world-class tourist destination in its own right. Buoyed by a tremendous economy, recent investments in infrastructure have put Lima on the map to stay. The City of Kings can now boast a series of fun and elegant activities that will delight and entertain any traveler, so extend your South American vacation a few more days and take part in all that Lima has to offer.
Walter Rhein

Lima

Between the fine dining, the dancing shows, and the beautiful water parks, Lima can be a great city to explore with that special someone. You're virtually guaranteed a pleasant evening as the weather in Lima is mild and perfect for an evening stroll. This itinerary will provide you with a nice mix of romantic excursions to make sure your stay over in Lima is a memorable one.

Alternative Tour

Lima

This tour is for those who are at the end of their vacation in Peru and are burned out on visiting traditional touristy things. Rather than check out more museums and traditional sites, perhaps you'd prefer to lounge by a pool and play some traditional Peruvian lawn games. This tour will take you to the outskirts of Lima where the residents of the city of kings go to relax after a long work week. These 36 hours will allow you to test your Spanish skills and see parts of Peru that most tourists never experience.

Lima

When it comes to food and drink, no nation in South America rivals Peru and no capital is quite like Lima – a melting pot of successive waves of immigration from Europe and Asia blend with indigenous influences. In Lima you have an almost endless list of top end gastronomy-related activities to keep the taste buds tingled.
